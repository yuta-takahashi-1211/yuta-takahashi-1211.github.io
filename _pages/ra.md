## Research Assistant Positions Available

We are actively recruiting motivated students interested in macroeconomics to join our research team as research assistants. If you are interested in contributing to cutting-edge economic research, please contact us.

---

## Macroeconomics Research Assistants

### Responsibilities:
- In-depth reading and critical analysis of academic papers
- Participating in regular discussions of current research
- Data analysis using AI tools and modern computational methods
- Literature reviews and synthesis of economic theory
- Supporting ongoing research projects

### Qualifications:
- Strong interest in macroeconomics
- Ability to read and comprehend academic papers
- Basic data analysis skills (or willingness to learn)
- Good communication skills in Japanese and English

---

## Microeconomics Theory Reading Group

We are also recruiting students with a strong interest in microeconomic theory, particularly those focused on theoretical papers that could have significant implications for macroeconomics.

If you have specific theoretical papers in mind that bridge microeconomics and macroeconomics, or if you are interested in exploring how microeconomic foundations inform macroeconomic phenomena, please contact us with:
- Your area of interest
- Relevant paper references
- Brief description of why these papers interest you

---

## Undergraduate Research Projects

### Historical Data Digitization Project

For undergraduate students interested in applied research work, we have an exciting project involving the digitization of historical data from physics textbooks. This project offers hands-on experience with:
- Historical data collection and management
- Data entry and quality control
- Understanding the intersection of physical science and economic history

If you are interested in this type of work and would like to contribute to building valuable historical datasets, please contact us.

## How to Apply

Please send an email to [yuta.takahashi.iser@osaka-u.ac.jp](mailto:yuta.takahashi.iser@osaka-u.ac.jp) with:
- Your name and current academic status
- Which position you are interested in
- A brief statement of your research interests
- Your CV (if available)

We look forward to hearing from motivated students who want to contribute to economic research!
